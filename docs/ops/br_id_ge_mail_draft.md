br_id_ge proposes a tele-symbiotic XR format in which two geographically distant performers co-create one shared performative body through an AI-mediated bridge.

The project operates as a four-part pipeline:
- Node A: Munich performer stream
- Node B: Gyumri performer stream
- AI Stitch: interpretation and translation layer
- Output: volumetric, choreographic, and audiovisual shared presence

Unlike standard remote performance tools, br_id_ge does not treat transmission as a neutral channel. Instead, network delay, packet loss, and temporal mismatch become part of the dramaturgy. The AI layer acts as a temporal and semantic mediator that maps movement and emotional signals into responsive visual and spatial forms.

Most remote systems preserve communication but flatten embodiment. br_id_ge explores how XR plus AI can preserve relational intensity between distant participants, creating new forms of artistic collaboration, learning, and public experience.

Potential impact areas:
- Arts and culture: new distributed performance grammar
- Education and labs: collaborative creative XR environments
- Research: human-machine co-presence and embodied interaction
- Creative technology: practical workflows for XR+AI co-creation

Current state:
The concept already exists as a working prototype and presentation environment, with modular expansion planned for additional cities, performers, and institutional collaborations.

Finale vision:
At Festival der Zukunft, we propose a live demonstration where two remote nodes and the AI Stitch layer produce one synchronized XR performance in real time, showing both the artistic and technical potential of distributed XR creation.
